Experiment Name: Verification of Stefan's Law

Theory

For black bodies, Stefan’s law is :-

E = σ (T4-T04) .................................(1)

Where E is the net amount of radiation emitted per second per unit area by a body at temperature T and surrounded by another body at temperature T0, σ is Stefan’s constant.

A similar relation can also hold for bodies that are not black. In such case, it can be written as..

P = C (Tα -T0α)......................................(2)

Where, P is the total power emitted by the body at temperature T, surrounded by another body at temperature T0 (figure 1), α is a power quite closed to 4 and C is some constant depending on the material and area of power radiating body.



Further the relation can be written as

P = C Tα (1-T0α/ Tα)............................................(3)

For large temperature of the body and very low temperature of surroundings the relation can be written as

P = C Tα..............................................................(4)

Taking logarithm of both the sides-

Log P = αLog T + Log C..........................................(5)

The graph between Log P and Log T should be a straight line (figure 2) whose slope gives α.
